🗓️ · WEEKLY PREVIEW: Golf This Week: Nelly Korda Chases History While the PGA Tour Closes Its Signature Season

BY GOLF RAW STAFF PUBLISHED WED 24 JUN

Quick answer: This is one of the biggest weeks of the 2026 golf season. Nelly Korda goes for her third straight major of the year at the KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine, June 25 to 28. On the men's side, the PGA Tour holds its final Signature Event of the season at the Travelers Championship, with Wyndham Clark arriving fresh off his U.S. Open win and Scottie Scheffler leading the field. Both events run the same Thursday-to-Sunday window. Here is your full guide to the week.

Here is your full guide to the week.

What Is the Biggest Story in Golf This Week?

Nelly Korda chasing history. After winning the first two majors of 2026, the Chevron Championship and the U.S. Women's Open, Korda arrives at Hazeltine with a chance to make it three straight majors in a single season, something no woman has done in the modern game.

She is the betting favorite, and for good reason. She has won four of her nine starts this year and has not finished worse than tied for eighth in any of them. A win would also clinch her spot in the LPGA Hall of Fame, since she sits just two points short, and move her to the top of the all-time LPGA career earnings list. It is hard to overstate how big a week this could be for her.

What Is the KPMG Women's PGA Championship?

It is the third of the five women's majors, and this year it carries a record-tying $12 million purse, level with the U.S. Women's Open as the richest in women's golf. The winner takes home $1.8 million.

The venue is Hazeltine National in Chaska, Minnesota, a long, demanding course that has hosted men's majors and Ryder Cups. The last time the Women's PGA was here, in 2019, Hannah Green won and Korda finished tied for third. Expect a tough test: narrow driving areas, deep rough, and large, well-protected greens that punish loose approaches.

Can Anyone Stop Korda at Hazeltine?

Yes, the field is deep. Korda is the favorite, not a lock, and several players can beat her.

  • Hannah Green won this event at Hazeltine in 2019 and has already won twice in 2026. She knows the course.
  • Minjee Lee is the defending champion, fresh off winning her third major last year.
  • Jeeno Thitikul, the world No. 2, is still hunting her first major and would love to grab it here.
  • Hyo Joo Kim has already beaten Korda twice this season, with Korda runner-up both times.
  • Lottie Woad, the 22-year-old phenom, has been one of the most consistent under-par scorers on tour.

A long, hard major on a course this good tends to produce a worthy champion. Korda is the story, but she will have to earn it.

What Is Happening on the PGA Tour This Week?

The Travelers Championship, the final Signature Event of the 2026 season, at TPC River Highlands in Cromwell, Connecticut, also June 25 to 28. It is a limited field of about 72 players competing for a $20 million purse, with $3.6 million and 700 FedExCup points going to the winner.

The timing is notable. It falls the week after the U.S. Open, so the same stars who just battled Shinnecock head straight to Connecticut for one more big payday before the schedule shifts toward The Open Championship. With no cut for most of the week and a stacked field, every round matters.

Who Is Playing the Travelers Championship?

Almost everyone who matters. World No. 1 Scottie Scheffler headlines, looking to bounce back after his U.S. Open Grand Slam bid fell short on his birthday. New U.S. Open champion Wyndham Clark arrives with momentum and a trophy. Defending champion Keegan Bradley, a New England favorite, is back after edging Tommy Fleetwood here last year.

The field also includes Cameron Young, Matt Fitzpatrick, Russell Henley, Justin Rose, Fleetwood, and Collin Morikawa. The one big absence is Rory McIlroy, who is skipping the event as part of a lighter 2026 schedule. The betting favorites are Scheffler, Xander Schauffele, and Ludvig Åberg.

Why Was This Such a Big Week for PGA Tour News?

Because the Tour reshaped its future. Away from the competition, the PGA Tour used the Travelers to announce the most significant structural changes in decades. Starting in 2028, the Tour will split into two concurrent series, a top-tier Championship Series and a feeder Challenger Series, with soccer-style promotion and relegation between them.

On the same day, the Tour confirmed that CEO Brian Rolapp will add the title of commissioner on January 1, 2027, taking over from the retiring Jay Monahan. And Tiger Woods, who chaired the committee behind the changes, made his first public appearance since a March DUI arrest and rehab stint to introduce the announcement. It was a landmark day for the business of golf, all packed into the Tour's final Signature Event week.

What Is Coming Up Next on the Golf Calendar?

The schedule heats up from here. After this week, the men's game turns toward the Genesis Scottish Open in early July and then the year's third men's major, The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale, July 16 to 19. The women follow their own major path, with the Amundi Evian Championship in France, July 9 to 12, as the fourth women's major of the year.

In other words, this week is a launching point into the heart of the summer major season. If Korda wins at Hazeltine, the Evian becomes a shot at a fourth straight major. If she does not, the chase resets. Either way, the next few weeks are loaded.

The Raw Read

This is the kind of week that reminds you how good golf can be when everything lands at once. The best player in women's golf is one tournament from history. The men are playing their richest regular event the week after a U.S. Open. And the sport itself just announced a future that will reshape how the whole thing works.

If you only have time for one thing, watch Korda at Hazeltine. A player going for three straight majors in a season does not come around often, and the story writes itself either way: history made, or a great chase that falls just short. The Travelers will deliver birdies and a fun finish as it always does, but the weight of the week sits on the women's side. This is Korda's moment to grab, and the rest of the field's job to spoil.
