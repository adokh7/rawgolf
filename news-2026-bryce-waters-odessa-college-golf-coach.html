· NEWS: Midland Christian Alum Bryce Waters Named Odessa College Golf Coach

BY GOLF RAW STAFF PUBLISHED FRI 26 JUN 2026 · 03:30 5 MIN READ
Quick Summary — The Key Facts
  • Who: Bryce Waters, a standout former multi-sport competitor at Midland Christian and Odessa College alum, has returned.
  • What: Named head coach of both the men's and women's golf programs at Odessa College.
  • When: The hire was announced by the school in February 2026.
  • Why: Recruiting experience, built-in familiarity with the school, and local West Texas pedigree offer a stable foundation for the Wrangler golf programs.

Hometown Return: Who Is Bryce Waters?

Waters isn't just another name on a coaching carousel; he is a product of Midland, Texas, who knows the local golf landscape inside and out. Coming up through Midland Christian, Waters was a multi-sport standout, proving his athletic pedigree across both basketball and golf. He earned district MVP and first-team all-state honors, culminating in a state championship during his senior year. Waters then went on to compete on the course for Odessa College. This background at the junior college level gave him first-hand experience of the program he now inherits. The athletic department's framing of his hiring as a "star returnee" highlights the lasting impression he left as a Wrangler player.

Setting the Direction: The Dual Role at Odessa College

Waters steps into the head coaching role for both the men's and women's programs at Odessa College. In junior college golf, dual head coaching duties are a common reality, requiring a coach to balance recruiting, travel, and practice schedules for two complete rosters. This dual responsibility gives Waters total control over the direction of Odessa College's golf program. The West Texas junior college scene has a deep golf pulse, characterized by intense local rivalries, most notably with nearby Midland College. Stepping into his alma mater gives Waters a significant advantage: an immediate, intimate familiarity with the school, the resources, and the West Texas recruiting trails.

Why the Alumni Connection Matters for Junior College Golf

In the high-turnover world of junior college athletics, stability is rare. Players cycle out in two years, and coaches frequently use these roles as stepping stones to NCAA Division I jobs. Hiring an alumnus like Waters counters this trend. An alum arrives with a pre-existing commitment to the school's culture and region. They recruit with genuine pride in the program, which resonates with prospects and their families. Furthermore, Waters's background as a competitor who has won state titles and competed at the collegiate level carries weight in the team room. Players respond to coaches who have stood in their shoes and succeeded.

The Raw Read: Results Over Resume

Hometown homecomings make for excellent press releases, but RawGolf's take remains grounded in the scoreboard: a coaching hire is a starting point, not a destination. Odessa College has made a logical, community-oriented choice by bringing back someone with genuine ties to West Texas golf and a reason to care about the Wrangler badge. However, the goodwill of a homecoming wears off quickly if the teams do not perform. Waters's tenure will not be judged by the announcement in February 2026, but by how his teams recruit, develop, and compete against rival programs over the coming seasons. The foundation is solid, but the real work starts on the course.
