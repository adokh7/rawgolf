DOCUMENT P-01 THE MANIFESTO STATUS PERMANENT

Golf deserves
better than
its coverage.

This is the most honest sport on earth. You sign your own card. You call penalties on yourself. You shoot what you shoot. So why does the media covering it read like it was written by a sponsor's legal team?

Part I — The Problem

Golf media became a PR machine.

Here's how it works, and everyone inside knows it: access is the currency. The interview, the locker-room pass, the seat in the press tent — all of it can be revoked. So the coverage gets careful. Then it gets soft. Then it gets useless.

A player tanks a Sunday and it's "a learning experience." A tour tears itself in half over money and it's "an evolving landscape." A swing falls apart on camera and nobody in the booth will say the word yips. The fan can see it. The data shows it. The coverage won't print it.

That's not journalism. That's hospitality.

X-1"He'll be disappointed with that one."  He made triple from the middle of the fairway.
X-2"Both sides remain optimistic."  They've hated each other for three years and it's about money.
X-3"He's working on some things."  He's lost 4 mph of ball speed and his coach.
X-4"A future star to watch."  His equipment deal was announced Tuesday.
They protect their credentials. We protect the card.
THE RAWGOLF MANIFESTO · LINE 1
Part II — The Answer

We cover golf like golf keeps score.

Rawgolf was built on one observation: the scorecard is the only piece of golf media that has never lied. It doesn't care about your sponsors, your story arc, or your media training. It records what happened, and it's final.

So that's the standard. Every story, every rating, every breakdown on this site is held to the scorecard's rules: earned numbers, no spin, signed and attested. We don't have press credentials to protect, because we never asked for any. Our access is the same as yours — the broadcast, the shot data, the sources who talk because they trust us — and it turns out that's enough to tell the truth.

Part III — The Promises

Four tenets.
Signed like a card.

These aren't values on a slide deck. They're rules we can be caught breaking — and the Corrections Log is where you'd catch us.

T1The Number Rule

The number is the story.

Every claim we publish traces back to something measurable: strokes gained, proximity, scoring under pressure, dollars and contracts. When we speculate, we label it speculation. When we're guessing, we say "we're guessing." Narrative serves the data — never the other way around.

VOW: IF WE CAN'T SOURCE IT OR MEASURE IT, WE DON'T PRINT IT.
T2The Access Rule

We owe access nothing.

No rating has ever been softened to keep an interview. No story has ever been spiked to stay friendly with a tour. If being honest about a player means that player never talks to us — that's a price, and we've already paid it. The reader is the only relationship we protect.

VOW: NO COVERAGE IS EVER TRADED FOR ACCESS. SEE RULE 4.2, DOCUMENT RG-001.
T3The Lock Rule

What we publish stays published.

Ratings lock at publication and never quietly change. Mistakes get corrected in the open — original visible, crossed out, dated, with credit to whoever caught it. We'd rather look wrong in public than be wrong in private. Memory-holing is the coward's edit.

VOW: EVERY ERROR LIVES FOREVER IN THE CORRECTIONS LOG. NEWEST FIRST.
T4The Fan Rule

Written by obsessives, for obsessives.

We assume you know what strokes gained means. We assume you've blown a Sunday lead of your own. We will never explain what a birdie is, never pad a story to hit a word count, and never write a headline the article can't cash. Your time is the green fee. We intend to be worth it.

VOW: NO FLUFF, NO FILLER, NO INSULTING YOUR GOLF IQ.

Who actually publishes this.

Every article, rating, and tool on this site is produced by GOLFRAW Editorial — the site's editorial desk. We attribute to the desk rather than to invented individual bylines, because we'd rather be honest about a team than manufacture a personality. If a named writer with a real, checkable track record ever joins, their byline will say so, backed by a real profile — not a stock photo and a fake job title.

GOLFRAW is independent: not owned by a tour, a broadcaster, or an equipment brand, and funded by reader advertising, not by anyone we cover.
