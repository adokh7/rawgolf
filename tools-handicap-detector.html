03 · How This Actually Works

How is a golf handicap calculated?

Every round becomes a Score Differential: (113 ÷ Slope Rating) × (Score − Course Rating). That converts a score shot on any course, of any difficulty, into a single comparable number. Your Handicap Index is then the average of your lowest differentials — 8 of your last 20 once you have a full record.

Why you never shoot your handicap

This is the part almost nobody understands. Your handicap is built from your best 8 rounds out of 20. It is a measure of your potential, not your average. Most golfers play to their index roughly one round in five. If you shoot it every week, you are not a good golfer — you are a bandit.

Vanity handicap vs bandit

A vanity handicapper claims a lower number than the maths supports, then quietly donates money every Saturday. A bandit claims a higher number than the maths supports and takes it. Both are lying. Only one of them is profiting.

Fewer than 20 rounds

The World Handicap System scales how many differentials it counts: with 5 rounds it uses your single lowest, with 6 it uses the lowest 2 minus an adjustment, and it climbs from there until you reach the full 8 of 20.

FAQ · Questions Golfers Actually Ask

How is a golf handicap calculated?

Each round produces a Score Differential of (113 / Slope Rating) x (Adjusted Gross Score - Course Rating). Your Handicap Index is the average of the lowest differentials from your most recent 20 scores, using 8 of 20 when you have a full record.

Why do I never shoot my handicap?

Because a handicap measures your potential, not your average. It is built from your best 8 rounds out of 20, so most golfers only play to their Handicap Index roughly one round in five.

How many scores do you need for a handicap?

A minimum of 54 holes, which is three 18-hole rounds. With 5 rounds only the single lowest differential is used, and the number of differentials counted rises as you post more scores until 8 of 20 is reached.
