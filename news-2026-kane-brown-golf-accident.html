⚠️ · ACCIDENT: Kane Brown Shares Photos After Golf Accident Leaves Him With a Concussion and 23 Stitches

BY GOLF RAW STAFF PUBLISHED WED 24 JUN

Quick answer: Country star Kane Brown was hit in the head by a golf ball, splitting his ear open and leaving him with 23 stitches and what he described as a slight concussion. The injury forced him to pull out at the last minute from a scheduled performance at the Tight Ends & Friends show in Nashville, part of Travis Kelce's Tight End University event. Brown shared photos and video of the injury with fans while recovering. He is expected to be okay.

Here is what happened, and why a stray golf ball is more dangerous than most people realize.

What Happened to Kane Brown?

Brown was struck in the head by a golf ball during what has been described as a freak accident on the course. The ball caught him on the ear, splitting it open badly enough to require 23 stitches. He went to the emergency room, where doctors diagnosed a slight concussion.

He shared the aftermath with fans directly, posting photos and video of the bloodied ear. The footage is graphic enough that several outlets ran warnings before showing it. For a musician whose livelihood depends on performing, a head injury like this is no small thing, and he made the call to rest rather than push through.

Did Kane Brown Cancel a Show Because of the Injury?

Yes. Brown was scheduled to perform at the Tight Ends & Friends show in Nashville, a high-profile event tied to Travis Kelce's Tight End University, and he pulled out at the last minute because of the accident.

It was a stacked lineup, with performances from Dan + Shay, Jon Pardi, Lainey Wilson, and others, plus appearances from NFL names like Kelce, George Kittle, and Greg Olsen. The night even featured a surprise Taylor Swift cameo during Lainey Wilson's set. Brown had been a memorable part of the same event in 2025, when he brought out Swift for a duet, so his late withdrawal was a genuine loss for the show. Given a concussion and 23 stitches, though, nobody could argue with the decision.

How Serious Is Getting Hit by a Golf Ball?

More serious than people assume, and that is the real lesson here. A golf ball is small and hard, and a struck shot can travel well over 100 miles per hour. Even a ball that has slowed down or taken a bounce carries enough force to cause deep cuts, concussions, broken bones, and worse when it hits someone in the head.

Brown's injury, an ear split open badly enough for 23 stitches plus a concussion, is a textbook example. It sounds like a fluke, and it was, but it is exactly the kind of fluke that happens on golf courses more often than you would think. The head and face are especially vulnerable, and there is rarely any time to react when a ball is coming.

How Can You Avoid Getting Hit by a Golf Ball?

A few simple habits prevent the vast majority of these accidents:

  • Never hit until the group ahead is well out of range. Most strike injuries happen because someone fired a shot too early. Patience is the single best protection.
  • Always yell "fore" immediately on an errant shot. It is not optional or old-fashioned. That shout gives people the split second they need to cover up or turn away.
  • If you hear "fore," protect your head and turn away. Do not look up to find the ball. Cover your head and neck and crouch. Looking for the ball is how people take one to the face.
  • Stay aware on shared and crossing holes. Many courses have spots where one hole runs close to another. Keep your head up in those zones.
  • Respect carts and walkers ahead. A ball can carry farther than you expect, especially downhill or downwind.

None of this guarantees safety, because freak accidents do happen, as Brown's case shows. But basic course etiquette exists precisely to keep stray golf balls from turning into ER trips.

Is Kane Brown Going to Be Okay?

By all indications, yes. A slight concussion and stitches, while painful and serious enough to cancel a show, are injuries people recover from with rest and proper care. He was treated, released, and is recovering at home.

The bigger caution with any concussion is the recovery, not the initial injury. Concussions need real rest, and rushing back to a demanding schedule of travel and loud, high-energy concerts would not be wise. The fact that Brown chose to sit out his Nashville performance suggests he and his team are taking it seriously, which is exactly the right call.

The Raw Read

It is easy to file this under celebrity news and move on, but there is a real golf lesson buried in it. Kane Brown is a fit, healthy guy who went out for what should have been a relaxing round and ended up in the emergency room with a split ear and a concussion. That is how fast a golf course can turn on you.

The takeaway is not to be scared of the game. It is to respect the simple safety habits that exist for exactly this reason. Wait until the group ahead is clear, shout "fore" the instant a shot goes sideways, and cover up when you hear it. Those few seconds of awareness are the difference between a funny story and 23 stitches. Here is hoping Brown heals up fast and gets back on stage soon, ideally with a new appreciation for yelling "fore."
