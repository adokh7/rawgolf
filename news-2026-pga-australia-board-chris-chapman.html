· TOUR GOVERNANCE: Chris Chapman Joins the PGA of Australia Board as Independent Director

BY GOLF RAW STAFF PUBLISHED THU 09 JUL

Quick Summary: The PGA of Australia has appointed former regulatory chief and corporate heavyweight Chris Chapman as an Independent Director to its board. Chapman, whose golf roots go back over 50 years at Royal Sydney Golf Club where he served as President, brings vast experience in sports media, venue management, and regulatory governance to the position. He replaces Ric Lazar, who steps down after 16 years of service.

A Strategic Changing of the Guard in Australian Golf

The PGA of Australia has announced a significant evolution in its administrative leadership with the appointment of Chris Chapman to its board as an Independent Director. Confirmed at the organization's recent Annual General Meeting, Chapman replaces Ric Lazar, who concluded an impressive 16-year term of service. A veteran corporate lawyer and highly respected sports administrator, Chapman represents a strategic addition to the governing body rather than a routine reshuffle, reflecting a clear institutional changing of the guard.

Unlike many boardroom appointments that select figures detached from the sport, Chapman possesses deep roots within Australian golf. His connection to Royal Sydney Golf Club spans over 50 years, tracing back to junior holiday clinics and Sunday caddying as a teenager in the 1960s. He eventually rose through the ranks to serve as President of the historic club, where he spearheaded the critical redevelopment of Royal Sydney’s championship layout. This blend of hands-on golf development and corporate oversight provides the PGA of Australia with a director who understands both the heritage of the game and the commercial realities of modern sports business.

At Golf Raw, our perspective remains firmly objective, stripped of the glossy language found in corporate press releases. Off-course governance rarely sets the headlines ablaze, but it dictates the financial health of the sport. Professional tours do not run on sponsor goodwill alone; they rely on regulatory navigation, broadcast rights valuations, and robust venue operations. Chapman's track record indicates he is uniquely equipped to optimize the tour's underlying business machinery.

Media, Regulation, and Venue Governance: The Strategic Fit

Chapman’s professional accolades represent an ideal fit for the commercial targets of the PGA of Australia. Most notably, he served two consecutive five-year terms as the inaugural Chairman of the Australian Communications and Media Authority (ACMA)—retained across both Liberal and Labor administrations. This bipartisan endorsement highlights a reputation for straight-shooting execution and strict regulatory adherence, a rare commodity in modern corporate governance.

Beyond his public sector achievements, Chapman’s commercial portfolio includes senior leadership roles at Seven Network and Stadium Australia, Sydney's Olympic showcase venue. Since 2016, he has worked in an advisory capacity for both the PGA of Australia and Golf Australia. Having already operated as an internal consultant, he enters the boardroom with an intimate understanding of the financial and administrative structures that run the professional tours down under.

PGA of Australia Board Structure

Following the recent AGM, the PGA of Australia board maintains a balanced structure designed to combine direct professional playing experience with outside corporate, legal, and financial expertise. The board is divided into Member Directors and Independent Directors:

  • Member Directors:
    • Ian Baker-Finch (Chair) — The 1991 Open Champion, directing overall board strategy.
    • Stephen Hutchison — Representing club professionals and member relations.
    • Chris Crocker — Focusing on golf facility management and member services.
    • Matt Griffin — Providing active touring professional perspectives.
  • Independent Directors:
    • Carole Brownlee — Advising on brand development and communications.
    • Alison Watkins AM — Bringing FMCG corporate strategy and investment experience.
    • Paul Lappin — Steering legal governance and financial compliance.
    • Chris Chapman — Directing media contracts, regulatory matters, and venue strategy.

Commercial Targets: Driving the Challenger Tour

PGA of Australia Chair Ian Baker-Finch made it clear where Chapman's skillset will be deployed. The board's current focus is centered on growing the Challenger PGA Tour of Australasia and the WPGA Tour of Australasia, alongside expanding education, vocational training, and member service programs. Navigating television deals, regional sponsorships, and municipal relations for developmental tours requires the precise commercial clout that Chapman has spent decades building.

For his part, Chapman’s public statements remained traditionally understated, expressing a desire to contribute directly to the next phase of the PGA’s strategic plan. However, the corporate history behind his appointment suggests his influence will be felt immediately behind closed doors. For those who watch the business of golf, Chapman’s addition is a highly logical step toward securing the long-term commercial sustainability of the game in the southern hemisphere.
