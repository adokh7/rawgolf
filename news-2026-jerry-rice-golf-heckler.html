· JERRY RICE HECKLER INCIDENT: 63-Year-Old Legend Sprints After Fan at Celebrity Golf Event

BY GOLFRAW EDITORIAL PUBLISHED THU 16 JUL

Quick Summary: Jerry Rice is 63 years old and last caught an NFL pass over two decades ago. But this week at the American Century Championship, the Hall of Famer sprinted after a spectator who heckled him mid-round. The confrontation reveals the famously fiery competitor hasn't lost his edge, adding another controversial Lake Tahoe chapter to his celebrity golf history.

What Did Jerry Rice Do?

He ran after a heckler on the golf course. A video posted by Zire Golf on Instagram shows Rice finishing a swing at the American Century Championship, a spectator yelling something in his direction, and Rice immediately taking off after the person. No hesitation. One swing, one shout, and the Hall of Famer was giving chase.

The caption said it plainly: someone heckled Jerry Rice, and he ran after him. What the fan actually shouted, and which round it happened in, hasn't been confirmed. So we're not going to guess. What's clear from the Jerry Rice golf video is that whatever was said, it was enough to make a 63-year-old legend break into a sprint across a fairway.

Where Did the Jerry Rice Heckler Incident Happen?

At the American Century Championship, golf's biggest celebrity tournament. The event runs every July at Edgewood Tahoe Golf Course in Stateline, Nevada, right on the shore of Lake Tahoe. This was the 37th edition, a 54-hole event with a $750,000 purse, broadcast on NBC and Peacock.

The field is stacked with famous names. This year's Lake Tahoe celebrity golf lineup included Stephen Curry, Charles Barkley, Tony Romo, Jason Kelce, Annika Sorenstam, and dozens more. Rice is a regular. He's been teeing it up at this thing alongside fellow 49ers legends for years. So the crowd knows him, and clearly at least one person in it knew exactly how to get under his skin.

How Did Jerry Rice Play at the ACC?

Not his best week, and that might be part of the story. Rice finished 42nd in the 90-player field with 72 total points under the tournament's modified Stableford format. He struggled badly in the final round, shooting 7-over par on the day.

A frustrating round and a heckler are a bad mix for a famously fiery competitor. Nobody's saying the two are linked, but anyone who's played golf knows how a rough round sharpens your temper. When you're already grinding and someone in the gallery decides to pipe up, the fuse gets short. Rice has never hidden his competitive edge, on the field or off it.

Is This the First Time Rice Has Lost His Cool?

No, and that's what makes this more than a one-off. Two years ago, at this same tournament, Rice made headlines for a different confrontation. In 2024, TMZ Sports reported he was caught on camera swearing at reporters who asked him about the Chiefs' wide receivers. Security had to step in that time.

So the Jerry Rice temper has surfaced at the ACC before. Put together, it paints a picture of a Hall of Famer who still carries the same fire that made him unstoppable for 20 NFL seasons, and who doesn't much enjoy being needled in public. The competitiveness that built the greatest receiving career in history didn't switch off when he retired. It just shows up on golf courses now.

Who Won the American Century Championship 2026?

While Rice was chasing hecklers, former tennis star Mardy Fish was busy winning. Fish captured his third ACC title with 72 points, holding off defending champion Joe Pavelski down the stretch. It's become one of the best crossover stories in celebrity golf, a former top-tier tennis pro turning into a serious threat on the course every summer.

The tournament remains a genuine highlight of the golf calendar for casual fans, mixing real competition with the fun of watching famous athletes and entertainers play the game the rest of us play. Big names, a beautiful course, and every so often, a Hall of Famer sprinting after a heckler.

The Raw Read

This is exactly why people love the American Century Championship. Once a year the biggest names in sport come down to our level, spraying tee shots and taking abuse from the gallery, same as anyone at a public course on a Saturday morning. Most of them grin and take it. Rice is not most of them.

The man is 63 and still won't let a bad round and a loud mouth slide, and honestly, good for him. That's the fire that caught 1,549 passes and won three Super Bowls, and it plainly never went anywhere. Whoever that fan was, he picked the wrong legend to shout at. And the rest of us got a cracking video out of it.
