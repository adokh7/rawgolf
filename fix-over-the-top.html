How to Fix an Over the Top Golf Swing: The Honest Way to Kill Your Slice

The coaching industry wants you to fix your swing path first. The data says that's backwards. Here is the unvarnished truth about why you come over the top—and how to permanently fix it.

BY RAWGOLF SWING DESK PUBLISHED MON 15 JUN 2026 6 MIN READ

If you slice the golf ball, you've heard the same generic advice from a dozen different sources. "Drop it in the slot." "Swing out to right field." "Tuck your right elbow."

It sounds logical. But let's look at the numbers: over 85% of amateur golfers struggle with an out-to-in swing path that produces a weak, glancing blow. Millions of lessons are given every year to fix it. Yet, the average handicap hasn't dropped in decades.

The traditional coaching industry has failed you. They are trying to fix the symptom instead of the disease.

The Big Lie: Path Before Face

When you swing "over the top," your club travels on an out-to-in path across the golf ball, imparting the brutal left-to-right spin that finds the right trees. Most instructors look at a video of this move and immediately try to force your arms to swing from the inside.

But the data from thousands of 3D motion captures reveals a much darker truth: Your over-the-top move is not a flaw. It is a highly intelligent, athletic compensation.

Think about it. If you have a wide-open clubface at the top of your backswing—and most amateurs do—what would happen if you actually managed to "drop it in the slot" and swing from the inside?

You would hit a 45-degree block directly into the houses on the right. Your brain is smarter than that. Subconsciously, your central nervous system knows the face is open. To prevent hitting the ball off the planet to the right, your brain forces your upper body to heave over the top, pulling the ball back toward the target.

"You don't come over the top because you have bad mechanics. You come over the top because it's the only way your brain knows how to keep the ball on the golf course with a wide-open clubface."
— RawGolf Swing Desk

The Honest Fix: Face Dictates Path

If you want to stop coming over the top, stop thinking about your swing path entirely. You need to earn the right to swing from the inside. And you earn that right by closing the clubface early.

1. The Motorcycle Move

At the top of your swing, you must bow your lead wrist (flexion). Imagine revving a motorcycle throttle with your left hand. This points the clubface toward the sky, effectively closing it relative to the arc.

2. Let Gravity Work

Once the face is shut, your brain will recognize that pulling over the top will result in a snap hook. As a natural survival mechanism, your body will instinctively shallow the shaft and drop your arms into the slot to prevent the hook.

You don't have to force the club inside. The moment you fix the clubface, the path will begin to fix itself.

The Verdict

Stop paying for lessons that force you to hit blocking drills with an open face. Fixing the path without fixing the face is like putting a band-aid on a bullet wound.

Close the face at the top. Trust the physics. And watch the slice disappear.
