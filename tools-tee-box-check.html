02 · Why This Matters

What tees should I play?

The simplest rule in golf: multiply your driver carry by 28. Carry it 220 and you belong on roughly a 6,160-yard course. Carry it 250 and you have earned 7,000 yards. Most amateurs are playing a course built for someone who hits it 40 yards past them.

The approach shot is the whole argument

Tee boxes exist so that different players face the same second shot. A par 4 is designed to be a drive and a mid-iron. If you are standing over 190 yards on every par 4 while the course architect intended a 9-iron, you are not playing the hole as designed — you are playing a much harder hole that nobody built.

Moving up is not cheating

It is the single cheapest improvement available to you. No lesson, no new driver, no range sessions. Just teeing off 500 yards further forward and suddenly hitting wedges into greens. Your handicap does not care which coloured post you started from.

How many shots is it really worth?

About 1.5 shots for every 200 yards you play beyond your ideal length. Play 800 yards too far back and you are handing away six shots before you hit a ball. That is more than most golfers gain from a winter of lessons.

FAQ · Questions Golfers Actually Ask

What tees should I play?

Multiply your driver carry distance by 28 to get your ideal course yardage. A player who carries their driver 220 yards should be playing a course of roughly 6,160 yards.

How many shots does playing the wrong tees cost you?

Roughly 1.5 shots for every 200 yards you play beyond your ideal course yardage. Playing 800 yards too far back costs about six shots a round.

Is moving up a tee box cheating?

No. Tee boxes are designed so that players of different distances face the same approach shots. Playing tees that are too long means hitting long irons into greens built for wedges.
