· NEWS: Phil Mickelson Steps Back as Misconduct Allegations Surface: Golf Digest Report and Alan Shipnuck Detail the Fallout

BY GOLF RAW STAFF PUBLISHED FRI 26 JUN 2026 · 23:03 8 MIN READ
Quick Answer

Phil Mickelson is facing a wave of misconduct allegations reported by journalist Alan Shipnuck and Golf Digest, including claims of inappropriate behavior toward women. Mickelson has denied wrongdoing, his attorney has called some of the accounts false, and at least one person reportedly present at one alleged incident has disputed it. It is critical to emphasize that these are allegations, several of which are contested, and not established findings of fact. Mickelson has reportedly lost or given up multiple club memberships and has stepped back from public life to deal with what his team describes as a private family matter.

The Allegations: Details From the Alan Shipnuck and Golf Digest Reports

A series of personal misconduct allegations against Phil Mickelson have emerged in recent weeks, driven by reporting from Golf Digest and Alan Shipnuck, the author of an unauthorized biography of the six-time major champion. The reports compile multiple separate accounts from women describing instances of inappropriate behavior spanning several years. While the details of these accounts are serious, they remain allegations rather than established findings of fact. Notably, unlike previous reporting that relied almost entirely on anonymous sources, at least one of the recent allegations has been publicly attached to a named individual, intensifying the spotlight on Mickelson's off-course conduct.

The reporting paints a picture of a legendary figure whose personal life is under intense scrutiny. Although out of respect for the parties involved the graphic specifics of the claims are not being relitigated, the cumulative weight of these reports has significantly impacted Mickelson’s public standing. The collection of separate accounts has shifted the conversation from his performance on the course to his behavior off it, forcing both fans and institutions to reckon with the claims.

The Response: Mickelson's Denials and Contested Accounts

Mickelson has firmly denied all allegations of wrongdoing, and his legal team has mounted a strong defense. His attorney, Tom Clare, issued a public statement asserting that several of the circulating accounts are entirely false, while others repackage past personal mistakes that Mickelson has already publicly acknowledged and apologized for. Clare argued that combining fresh, contested allegations with previously addressed issues creates a distorted and misleading narrative of the golfer's character.

Crucially, some elements of the reported events are disputed by third parties. For instance, in one of the highlighted incidents involving an alleged dinner conversation, a person who was reportedly present at the table—and who happened to be the accuser’s own representative at the time—publicly denied that the events occurred as described. This highlight demonstrates that the underlying facts of these cases remain highly contested, rather than agreed upon by all parties involved.

Elite Club Departures: Disputed Resignations and Exits

The fallout from the allegations has extended to Mickelson's relationship with several elite golf institutions. According to reports from Golf Digest, Mickelson parted ways with The Farms Golf Club in Rancho Santa Fe, California, this spring. The departure followed an accusation by a female clubhouse employee of inappropriate physical contact. The reports also reference departures from other prominent clubs, highlighting a significant shift in his access to the elite social circles he once frequented.

Mickelson's representatives and legal team have challenged the narrative of him being expelled from these institutions. His attorney stated that at least one of these exits was a voluntary resignation, framing it as a choice by Mickelson to step away on his own terms rather than a disciplinary removal. Regardless of the official designation, the practical outcome remains the same: one of the most famous players in golf history is no longer a member of clubs where he was once a fixture.

A Silent Community: The Golf World's Reaction

The reaction from the broader golf community has been characterized by extreme caution and public silence. In stark contrast to earlier periods of his career, there has been no rush of public defense from fellow players or former peers, illustrating Mickelson's growing isolation within the professional ranks. Major sponsors, corporate partners, and governing bodies have also chosen to remain quiet, refraining from issuing public statements or taking overt disciplinary actions while the claims remain unresolved.

This quiet reaction can be interpreted in two ways. On one hand, it highlights the natural distance organizations maintain during active reputational crises. On the other hand, it reflects the legal and ethical complexity of a situation involving serious, contested allegations. For now, the golf world is choosing to observe from a distance, waiting for more established facts or formal proceedings before taking a definitive stance.

The Broader Decline: From Fan Favorite to the Margins of Golf

Mickelson's current isolation is not a sudden collapse, but rather the latest and most severe chapter in a multi-year decline. For decades, Mickelson was one of the most adored figures in sports—a swashbuckling, thumbs-up-flashing icon who commanded massive galleries and corporate backing. However, that goodwill began draining away in 2022 following his highly controversial comments regarding Saudi Arabia's human rights record, which he made while explaining his pivot to the LIV Golf league. The remarks drew widespread condemnation and forced him into a temporary hiatus from the game.

Additionally, Mickelson's public transparency regarding his long-term struggles with gambling had already introduced fractures in his once-pristine image. Because his reputation was already fragile, the emergence of these misconduct allegations has hit harder than they might have at the peak of his popularity. Rather than tarnishing a clean slate, they have accelerated a decline that has moved him further to the margins of the sport.

The Raw Read: Taking the Claims and Denials Seriously

Responsible reporting of this story requires acknowledging the discomfort of an unresolved situation. Serious allegations have been brought forward by named and unnamed individuals, and equally serious denials have been issued by Mickelson and corroborated in part by third-party witnesses. Treating the matter as settled in either direction is a disservice to the facts. The only objective truth at this stage is that Phil Mickelson's legacy is in a state of profound crisis.

A player who once stood at the center of the golf world now watches from the sidelines, having stepped back to address private family matters. Whether the truth is ultimately sorted out in the court of public opinion, institutional hearings, or legal challenges, the current moment represents a somber transition for one of golf's most decorated careers. Until formal evidence is presented and tested, the most responsible position remains a measured one: evaluate the allegations, respect the denials, and wait for the facts.
