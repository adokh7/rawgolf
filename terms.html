The rules for using golfraw.com, written the way we'd explain them across a table rather than the way a law firm would print them. The formal bits are here because they have to be — everything else is here because you'd actually want to know it.

§1

What you're agreeing to

By using golfraw.com — reading a story, running a tool, signing up for the newsletter — you're agreeing to these terms. If you don't agree, the straightforward option is to not use the site. Nobody's making you.

We may update these terms as the site grows. If a change is material, we'll flag it the same way we handle every other correction: openly, with a new date at the top and a note in the newsletter. No silent rewrites.

We are not affiliated with the PGA Tour, LPGA, DP World Tour, the R&A, the USGA, or any player, team, or brand we cover. The site is funded by advertising (Google AdSense) and, eventually, reader newsletter engagement. It costs nothing to read, and the tools cost nothing to use — see Privacy Policy for exactly what that means for your data.

The tools: what they are, and what they are not

Every calculator in the Raw Tools suite — The Settle Up, The Round Autopsy, The Tee Box Reality Check, The Handicap Lie Detector, The Plays Like Calculator, The Tilt Meter, The Bag Audit, The Gimme Audit — runs entirely in your browser. No account, no server-side processing, no data uploaded. Full technical detail is in the Privacy Policy.

  • The tools are estimates, not professional advice. They apply published formulas and reasonable models to the numbers you enter. They are not a substitute for a certified coach, a fitting session, a handicap committee, or a doctor.
  • The betting calculator settles money between consenting adults playing a friendly game. It does not facilitate wagering, does not process payments, and is not gambling software. What your group does with the numbers it produces is between you and your group, and your local laws.
  • Handicap-related tools are informational only. The Handicap Lie Detector applies the published World Handicap System differential formula for entertainment and self-audit purposes. It is not a substitute for an official handicap issued through a recognised golf association, and we make no claim that its output is valid for handicap purposes.
  • We are not liable for decisions made using tool output. If The Tee Box Reality Check tells you to move up a tee and your Saturday four-ball has opinions about that, that's between you and your four-ball.
Plain English The tools do real math on numbers you give them. Use the answers as a starting point for a conversation, not as the final word from an authority.
