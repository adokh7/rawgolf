· TOURNAMENT RECAP: Hovland Beats Scheffler in a Monday Playoff to Win the Travelers, and a Two-Footer Decided It

BY GOLF RAW STAFF PUBLISHED TUE 30 JUN 2026 · 00:05 6 MIN READ
Quick Summary

Viktor Hovland captured the 2026 Travelers Championship on Monday morning, defeating Scottie Scheffler on the first hole of a sudden-death playoff at TPC River Highlands. After weather delays and darkness forced a Monday finish, the two ball-striking giants returned to play the par-4 18th. Hovland rolled in a clutch seven-foot birdie putt, putting the pressure back on Scheffler. In a shocking twist, the world No. 1 missed a routine two-foot, four-inch birdie putt to extend the playoff, handing Hovland his eighth PGA Tour win and his first victory in 15 months.

The Monday Finish: Pressure Cooker at TPC River Highlands

TPC River Highlands has witnessed its share of dramatic finishes, but the 2026 Travelers Championship delivered a conclusion that was as shocking as it was sudden. Heavy storms on Sunday afternoon caused an 83-minute weather delay, pushing the final group deep into the Connecticut twilight. By the time Hovland and Scheffler finished their 72nd holes tied at 21-under par, darkness had enveloped the course, leaving no choice but to call for a Monday morning playoff. It was the first Monday playoff finish on the PGA Tour since Rory McIlroy defeated J.J. Spaun at the 2025 Players Championship, setting the stage for a high-intensity, micro-session of golf where a single mistake would prove fatal.

Sudden-death playoffs are as much about mental endurance as they are about physical execution. Returning to a quiet, morning-dew-covered course after a night of anticipation shifts the competitive dynamic. There is no time to warm into the round; players must produce elite golf immediately. For Hovland and Scheffler, the two premier ball-strikers of their generation, the playoff was a test of who could execute under maximum psychological pressure.

A Shocking Twist on the 18th Green

Both players executed flawless tee shots down the fairway of the par-4 18th hole. Playing first from the fairway, Scheffler hit a brilliant 8-iron approach that settled just two feet and four inches from the cup. It was a trademark piece of iron play from the world No. 1, drawing a thunderous roar from the early-morning gallery and putting immediate, immense pressure on Hovland. The tournament appeared to be Scheffler’s to lose, with a near-certain birdie carded.

Hovland, however, refused to blink. Standing over his approach wedge, he spun a dart to seven feet, ensuring he would at least have a look to push Scheffler. Knowing that a miss would end his week, Hovland stepped up and confidently rolled the birdie putt into the center of the cup. That putt shifted the entire psychological landscape. Suddenly, Scheffler was not putting for the win, but rather to extend the playoff. In one of the most stunning misses in recent golf history, Scheffler pushed his short putt. The ball grazed the left lip and slipped by, leaving the world No. 1 in disbelief and crowning Hovland the champion.

The Psychology of the Short Putt and Scheffler's Agony

In professional golf, short putts are rarely about technique; they are about commitment and nerves. Under normal circumstances, a two-foot putt is a formality for Scottie Scheffler. Yet, the sudden shift in momentum—from thinking he had won the tournament with a brilliant approach to suddenly needing to convert to survive—can derail even the most disciplined minds. The push was a rare vulnerability from a player who has dominated the sport, demonstrating that even the best in the world are susceptible to the intense pressure of a playoff environment.

Despite the agonizing finish, Scheffler remained objective about his performance. This marked his fourth runner-up finish of 2026, leaving him with a single victory on the season despite statistical dominance. While a stack of second-place finishes would satisfy most, it represents a frustrating drought for Scheffler. Nonetheless, he noted that his ball-striking felt as sharp as it had all season, providing strong momentum as the field prepares for the Open Championship at Royal Birkdale.

Hovland’s Gritty Rally and Redemption

Hovland’s path to the playoff was defined by a remarkable back-nine comeback on Sunday. His bid for the title seemed lost when a bogey on the 10th hole, combined with a Scheffler birdie, created a two-shot deficit. Against Scheffler, a two-stroke lead is typically insurmountable. However, Hovland rallied with three consecutive birdies on the 13th, 14th, and 15th holes, showing immense composure directly after the long rain delay. His iron play, which had been inconsistent for months, came alive when it mattered most.

This victory represents a massive milestone for the 28-year-old Norwegian. Having struggled with swing changes and confidence issues over the past 15 months, Hovland's triumph at the Travelers is a validation of his hard work. Doing so in front of his mother, Galina—who witnessed his PGA Tour win in person for the first time—and his dedicated Norwegian fans made the moment unforgettable. Hovland proved that when his ball-striking is dialed in, he can go toe-to-toe with the very best and win.

A Signature Leaderboard and What Lies Ahead

The leaderboard reflected the premium nature of the PGA Tour's final Signature Event of 2026. Collin Morikawa finished in solo third at 20-under par after firing a sensational, career-low-tying 61 to charge from eight shots back. Matt Fitzpatrick claimed fourth at 19-under, while newly crowned U.S. Open champion Wyndham Clark finished fifth at 18-under. The star-studded finish justified the Signature Event model, delivering a high-stakes duel between major champions.

With the Travelers Championship in the books, the golf world turns its attention to the final major of the year. Both Hovland and Scheffler leave Connecticut with their games trending in the right direction, setting up what promises to be a thrilling battle at Royal Birkdale.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who won the 2026 Travelers Championship?

Viktor Hovland won the 2026 Travelers Championship, defeating Scottie Scheffler on the first hole of a sudden-death playoff on Monday morning at TPC River Highlands.

What was the final score of the tournament?

Both Viktor Hovland and Scottie Scheffler finished the 72 holes of regulation play tied at 21-under par before entering the playoff.

Why did the Travelers Championship finish on Monday?

An 83-minute rain delay during Sunday's final round pushed the play late into the evening. By the time regulation play concluded, there was insufficient daylight to hold the playoff, forcing a Monday morning restart.

How did Viktor Hovland win the playoff?

On the first playoff hole (the par-4 18th), Hovland made a seven-foot birdie putt. Scottie Scheffler then missed a short birdie putt from just over two feet that would have extended the playoff.

How long had it been since Hovland's last PGA Tour victory?

It had been 15 months since Hovland's last victory, which came at the 2025 Valspar Championship. This win marks the eighth PGA Tour title of his career.

Who else finished at the top of the leaderboard?

Collin Morikawa finished third at 20-under par after a brilliant closing 61. Matt Fitzpatrick finished fourth at 19-under, and U.S. Open champion Wyndham Clark finished fifth at 18-under.
