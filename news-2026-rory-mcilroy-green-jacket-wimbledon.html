· LIFESTYLE & CULTURE: Rory McIlroy Wore His Green Jacket to Wimbledon, and Somehow It Worked

BY COLIN HAYES PUBLISHED THU 02 JUL 2026 · 01:30 4 MIN READ
Quick Summary

Rory McIlroy turned up in the Royal Box at Wimbledon on Wednesday wearing his Masters green jacket, pairing golf's most exclusive garment with Centre Court's strictest dress code. The two-time defending Masters champion sat with his wife Erica and half of Europe's victorious Ryder Cup team, including Tommy Fleetwood, Justin Rose and captain Luke Donald, to watch world No. 1 Jannik Sinner win his second-round match. It was a cheeky, brilliant bit of flexing from a man who spent a decade chasing that jacket, and the crowd loved it.

A Masters Flex in the Royal Box

The green jacket from Augusta National is arguably the most exclusive garment in sports. It is a symbol of decades of history, grueling major championship pressure, and ultimate golfing success. On day three of The Championships at Wimbledon, Rory McIlroy decided that this ultimate prize was the perfect option for a afternoon out at Centre Court. Sat front and center in the prestigious Royal Box, McIlroy paired the bright green blazer with a crisp white shirt and a matching green tie, looking every inch like a man who knew exactly what kind of style statement he was making.

Wimbledon is famous for its strict, almost puritanical on-court dress code, where players are mandated to wear nothing but white. By introducing a massive splash of Augusta National green right into the VIP seats, McIlroy pulled off the ultimate crossover flex. It was bold, it was cheeky, and it was entirely impossible to miss.

Protocol, Privilege, and the Ultimate Green Jacket Rule

What makes McIlroy’s fashion statement even more delicious is the strict protocol surrounding the green jacket. According to Augusta National rules, only the reigning Masters champion is permitted to remove the jacket from the club grounds, and they must return it before the next tournament. Everyone else’s jacket—including those of former champions—remains permanently locked away at the club, only to be worn during visits.

By donning the jacket at Centre Court, McIlroy was exercising a privilege that precisely one golfer on earth currently holds. Having successfully defended his Masters crown this past April, Rory remains the sole keeper of the green blazer outside of Georgia. He clearly has no intention of letting that privilege go to waste, choosing to show it off in one of the most high-profile settings in the sporting summer.

A Ryder Cup Reunion on Centre Court

McIlroy was not alone in his afternoon at the tennis. The Royal Box on Wednesday looked like a celebration of European team golf, packed with the core of the Ryder Cup team that defeated the United States at Bethpage Black last autumn. Sitting alongside Rory and his wife Erica were Tommy Fleetwood, Justin Rose, Edoardo and Francesco Molinari, captain Luke Donald, and vice-captain Thomas Bjorn.

The group watched defending champion Jannik Sinner dispatch Nuno Borges in straight sets on the grass. Afterward, McIlroy met and chatted with the world No. 1. With The Open at Royal Birkdale just a fortnight away, the Ryder Cup team clearly relished a day out to support another sport while being celebrated as champions on tennis turf. The chemistry and lighthearted mood of the group were on full display, with Rory and Erica snapped sharing a kiss between matches.

The Long Road and a Good-Humored Playoff Reminder

The sight of McIlroy grinning in his green jacket is a massive shift from the narrative that defined the previous decade of his career. Rory spent eleven years chasing the Masters title, needing only that victory to complete the career Grand Slam. That chase became the defining hurdle of his career, filled with close calls and agonizing press conferences.

His breakthrough in 2025—where he beat Justin Rose in a dramatic playoff—finally lifted the curse. Then, in April 2026, he proved it was no fluke by holding off Scottie Scheffler by a single shot at 12-under par to win back-to-back Masters. The seating arrangement at Wimbledon added a layer of irony to the day: sitting right near Rory in the Royal Box was Justin Rose, the very man he defeated to claim his first green jacket. The golf world is small, and fortunately, it is good-humored enough to appreciate the irony.

Satisfying Wimbledon's Strictest Dress Code

For those wondering if McIlroy ran afoul of Wimbledon’s legendary dress codes, the answer is a resounding no. While the players on court are bound to all-white attire, the Royal Box guests are simply required to wear smart dress, which for men means a jacket and tie. Comedian Andy Samberg was famously reprimanded in the past for attempting to wear black jeans, proving the dress code is strictly enforced.

Technically, McIlroy’s single-breasted Augusta National blazer satisfies the requirement perfectly, no matter how loud the green hue might be. Rory found the one blazer on earth that outranks any standard dress code, at the one sporting event stuffy enough to care. It was the ultimate loophole flex: fully compliant, yet completely impossible to upstage.

The Raw Read: A Victory Lap Well Earned

Some athletes win a major title and immediately tuck the trophy away in a safe cabinet. McIlroy waited over a decade for a green jacket, and he is choosing to wear it to the tennis. Frankly, that is the exact response golf fans should want to see. The reign of a Masters champion is brief, lasting only until next April, and McIlroy is plainly determined to wring every drop of joy out of his victory lap.

The honest read is that this crossover moment was harmless, funny, and quietly iconic. Seeing a Masters green jacket in the Wimbledon Royal Box, surrounded by Ryder Cup legends just weeks before an Open Championship on English soil, is about as concentrated as a British sporting summer can get. If McIlroy brings this same relaxed, confident swagger to Royal Birkdale, the Claret Jug might be joining his wardrobe next.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did Rory McIlroy wear the green jacket at Wimbledon?

As the reigning Masters champion, McIlroy is the only golfer allowed to take the green jacket off the grounds of Augusta National. He wore it in the Wimbledon Royal Box to celebrate his status and enjoy a cheeky style flex while watching the tennis.

Who was with McIlroy in the Royal Box?

He was accompanied by his wife Erica and several members of Europe's victorious Ryder Cup team, including Tommy Fleetwood, Justin Rose, Edoardo and Francesco Molinari, captain Luke Donald, and vice-captain Thomas Bjorn.

Is McIlroy allowed to keep the green jacket permanently?

No. Masters champions are only allowed to keep the green jacket in their personal possession during their reigning year. Afterward, the jacket must be returned to Augusta National, where it remains in the clubhouse for their exclusive use during visits.

How many Masters tournaments has McIlroy won?

McIlroy has won two Masters titles, back to back. He won his first in 2025 by defeating Justin Rose in a playoff to complete the career Grand Slam, and successfully defended his title in April 2026 by one shot over Scottie Scheffler.

Did the green jacket break Wimbledon's dress code?

No. Wimbledon's all-white dress code only applies to players competing on the court. Royal Box guests must follow a smart-dress protocol requiring jackets and ties for men, which McIlroy's Augusta blazer fully satisfied.

What major tournament is next for Rory McIlroy?

McIlroy will head to Royal Birkdale for the 154th Open Championship, taking place from July 16 to 19, 2026, where he will attempt to secure his second major victory of the year.
