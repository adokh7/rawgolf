02 · What The Number Actually Means

Tilt is a measurable thing, not a personality trait

Tilt is borrowed from poker. It describes playing worse because you are rattled, rather than because the game in front of you got harder. Golf is the most tiltable sport ever invented: there is a five minute gap between the disaster and your next chance to do anything about it, and nobody to blame.

The baseline

Your baseline is the median hole of your round with your single worst hole removed. Median rather than average, because one nine on a par four drags an average so far off that every other hole starts to look like good golf. Removing the blow-up matters too — otherwise the disaster you are measuring quietly raises the bar you are being measured against, and everyone looks calm.

The tilt window

The three holes immediately after your worst hole. If you were a robot, those three would cost you three times your baseline. Anything above that is the meltdown cost: shots you dropped on holes that were no harder than the ones you had been handling all day.

Bounce-back

The tour stat. What share of your double bogeys or worse were followed straight away by a par or better? Tour professionals live around 20%. Club golfers are nearer 10%. It is the cleanest single measure of whether a bad hole stays one bad hole.

The honest limitation

This tool does not know your course. A blow-up on the 7th followed by three holes into a stiff breeze will read as tilt when it was really the wind, and a run of short par fours after your disaster will flatter you. Over a single card it is a conversation starter. Over ten cards, the pattern is real — which is why every run you do is saved to your dashboard.

FAQ · Questions Golfers Actually Ask

What does tilt mean in golf?

Tilt is a poker term that describes playing worse because you are emotionally rattled rather than because the challenge got harder. In golf it shows up as the extra shots you drop in the holes immediately after a blow-up, when the hole in front of you is no harder than the ones you were parring an hour earlier.

What is a good bounce-back rate in golf?

Bounce-back measures how often you follow a double bogey or worse with a par or better. Tour professionals sit around 20 percent and club amateurs are closer to 10 percent. Anything above 20 percent means you reset faster than most people who play golf for a living.

How do you stop tilting on the golf course?

The measurable fix is to treat the next tee shot as the most conservative shot of your round. Most meltdown damage comes from trying to win the lost shots straight back with an aggressive line off the following tee, which turns one bad hole into three.

How is the Tilt Index calculated?

The Tilt Index combines two measurements. First, the strokes you dropped across the three holes after your worst hole compared with your median hole for the round, excluding that worst hole. Second, your bounce-back rate across every double bogey or worse in the round. The two are weighted 55 and 45 percent and scaled to a score out of ten.
