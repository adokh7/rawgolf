· POWER RANKINGS: Genesis Scottish Open Power Rankings: The Renaissance Club's Contenders Ranked

BY GOLF RAW STAFF PUBLISHED TUE 07 JUL

Quick Summary: The 2026 Genesis Scottish Open (July 9-12 at The Renaissance Club) draws a ferocious field, including 10 of the world's top 15 players, just one week before the final major of the year. While world No. 1 Scottie Scheffler enters as the heavy betting favorite, Rory McIlroy's staggering track record at North Berwick makes him the field's most logical threat. Local hero Robert MacIntyre, a flat-flighted Ludvig Åberg, and seaside specialist Matt Fitzpatrick round out our elite contenders built to withstand the biting coastal wind.

The Tactical Challenge of The Renaissance Club

The Renaissance Club in North Berwick represents a modern hybrid test: a long hitter's links, but not a pure, classic sandy dune run. Architect Tom Doak designed generous landing areas off the tee, but the course places a premium on controlled, long-iron play into massive greens. When the coastal wind begins to bite off the Firth of Forth, player execution becomes the ultimate metric. It is not as savage as a traditional Open layout, but it acts as the perfect final preparation before Royal Birkdale next week.

Analyzing recent leaderboards reveals a clear blueprint for success. Winners here must possess a combination of explosive driving distance, flat-trajectory control in crosswinds, and elite scrambling skills. The last three champions all crossed the 15-under mark, indicating that while scoreable conditions exist, only power players with a sharp short game can maintain momentum when the weather turns foul. Here is how the top contenders rank for the 2026 edition.

1. Rory McIlroy

Rory McIlroy is the most logical course-fit pick in the field. McIlroy simply owns The Renaissance Club. His track record is unmatched: he claimed the trophy in 2023, finished as the runner-up last year, and notched a T4 in 2024. The course layout caters perfectly to his strengths, allowing him to launch high-draw drives into wide fairways and flight long irons through heavy crosswinds. Statistically, McIlroy continues to lead the field in Strokes Gained off the tee this season.

The primary concern surrounding McIlroy is competitive sharpness. He has maintained a light summer schedule and may display initial tournament rust. However, his putting performance in 2026 has quietly improved, turning what was once a liability into a key asset. When McIlroy finds his rhythm on a course he has already mastered, his floor is higher than almost anyone else in golf.

2. Scottie Scheffler

Scottie Scheffler is the best player in the world, yet he arrives in Scotland trying to break a puzzling winless stretch. Scheffler enters as the betting favorite, but he hasn't hoisted a trophy since January's American Express. Despite this, his consistency remains remarkable: he has secured four solo-second finishes in his last eight starts, including two playoff defeats. Most recently, he missed a crucial short putt in a Monday playoff at the Travelers, handing the win to Viktor Hovland.

While skeptics might view his playoff misses as a warning sign, historical context suggests Scheffler is a coiled spring. He used this exact tournament in 2025 as his springboard to major glory, finishing T8 in North Berwick before capturing the Open Championship. His tee-to-green metrics remain historically dominant, and he is simply too good to be kept out of the winner's circle much longer.

3. Robert MacIntyre

Robert MacIntyre represents the emotional heartbeat of the tournament. The local hero has written a spectacular history at this venue, finishing runner-up to McIlroy in 2023 before executing a brilliant closing birdie to steal the title from Adam Scott in 2024. Competing in front of a passionate home crowd, MacIntyre has shown that he thrives under the unique pressure of his national open.

Beyond sentimentality, MacIntyre's game is built for these conditions. He ranks highly in off-the-tee gains and handles volatile coastal winds with the natural instincts of someone who grew up on Scottish links. Coming off strong major performances earlier this season, MacIntyre possesses both the local knowledge and the athletic form to contend for a second Scottish Open crown.

4. Ludvig Åberg

Ludvig Åberg is the prototype for modern links success. The young Swede combines elite driving distance with a remarkably flat ball flight, allowing him to control his trajectory when the coastal wind picks up. This mechanical advantage makes him a dual-threat: he can pile up birdies during calm morning waves and comfortably grind out pars when afternoon gusts arrive.

For Åberg, the key to victory lies in minimizing unforced errors on the greens and avoiding the occasional loose iron swing. If he can maintain a steady putter, his ball-striking baseline is more than enough to dismantle Doak's design and put him in the mix on Sunday afternoon.

5. Matt Fitzpatrick

Matt Fitzpatrick is the ultimate competitor for seaside conditions. Fitzpatrick has put together a stellar 2026 campaign, capturing three PGA Tour wins—including a gritty playoff victory over Scheffler at Harbour Town. His record at The Renaissance Club is exceptionally stable, featuring finishes of T2, T4, and T6, alongside a victory at the 2023 Alfred Dunhill Links.

While Fitzpatrick lacks the raw driving distance of McIlroy or Åberg, he compensates with precise long-iron execution and exceptional fairway-finding ability. On a long links-adjacent course, his scrambling and elite short-game metrics keep him in contention. Dismissing his chances in windy, coastal conditions is always a mistake.

Who Else Could Contend?

The field's depth is extraordinary, leaving several high-profile names just outside the top five. Tyrrell Hatton has a stellar track record at this course, never finishing worse than T24, and historically thrives in cold, wet weather. Nicolai Hojgaard has finished T4 and T6 here, making him a prime candidate for a European breakthrough. Kurt Kitayama, a past runner-up, boasts ball-striking numbers that rival the top tier of the field.

Jon Rahm brings major pedigree and formidable power, while defending champion Chris Gotterup seeks to recreate his 2025 magic. Viktor Hovland, riding high after his Travelers playoff victory, and former champion Xander Schauffele add further star power. When you factor in major winners like Justin Thomas, Wyndham Clark, and links specialist Tommy Fleetwood, the leaderboard could shift in a dozen directions come Sunday.

The Raw Read

The defining truth of the Scottish Open is that the coastal wind remains the ultimate equalizer, and tee-time waves can dictate a tournament's outcome as much as raw talent. A sudden storm front on Thursday or Friday can completely derail a favorite's championship aspirations. For this reason, targeting players with established, multi-year track records at The Renaissance Club—such as McIlroy, MacIntyre, and Fitzpatrick—is far more logical than chasing short-term momentum.

If forced to pick a single champion, Rory McIlroy is the choice. His driver remains the field's most lethal weapon, his course history is flawless, and his putter has shown the necessary consistency in 2026. However, golf fans should prepare for a potential MacIntyre repeat or a Scheffler breakthrough, as both players are primed to peak just in time for Royal Birkdale.

Frequently Asked Questions

When and where is the 2026 Genesis Scottish Open?

The tournament is scheduled for July 9-12, 2026, at The Renaissance Club in North Berwick, Scotland. The event is co-sanctioned by the PGA Tour and DP World Tour, offering a $9 million purse.

Who is the betting favorite to win the Scottish Open?

Scottie Scheffler is the betting favorite, followed closely by Rory McIlroy. Jon Rahm and Ludvig Åberg lead the next tier of contenders.

Who is the defending champion at The Renaissance Club?

Chris Gotterup is the defending champion, having won the 2025 Genesis Scottish Open by two strokes to claim his second career PGA Tour victory.

Why does the field feature both PGA Tour and DP World Tour players?

As a co-sanctioned event, the field is split between both tours. This strategic placement makes it a premier warm-up event for elite players competing in the Open Championship the following week.

What type of player thrives at The Renaissance Club?

Success requires excellent driving distance, controlled iron play in high winds, and strong scrambling skills around large greens. Recent champions have all paired power off the tee with sharp short games.

Does the Scottish Open offer qualifying spots for the Open Championship?

Yes. The top three players who make the cut and are not already exempt will earn spots in the field at Royal Birkdale next week.
