· NEWS: Ryan Stefko Named Assistant Men's and Women's Golf Coach at Northern Illinois

BY GOLFRAW EDITORIAL PUBLISHED SUN 28 JUN 2026 · 16:15 4 MIN READ
Quick Answer

Northern Illinois University has hired Ryan Stefko as the assistant coach for both its men's and women's golf programs. Stefko joins the Huskies after three seasons in the same dual role at Menlo College in California, where both teams won tournaments and produced conference honorees. A 2020 Marquette graduate, he brings collegiate coaching experience and a player-development focus to DeKalb.

Who Is Ryan Stefko? A Rising Development Coach

Ryan Stefko arrives at Northern Illinois University with a robust background in collegiate athlete development. Serving most recently as the assistant coach for both the men's and women's teams at Menlo College, Stefko spent three seasons refining his approach to player instruction and team dynamics. A 2020 graduate of Marquette University, his path into the coaching ranks began in 2021, when he coached high school junior varsity golf and served as an assistant football coach at Sacred Heart Cathedral Preparatory in San Francisco.

Stefko’s experience extends beyond traditional team structures. He has worked as a lead instructor at Stanford's summer golf camps and participated in US collegiate golf development clinics in China. This diverse teaching exposure has allowed him to develop a coaching style focused on open communication, trust, and individual technical improvement. He describes his leadership philosophy as authentic and conversational, aiming to build lasting player-coach relationships that translate into competitive stability on the course.

A Dual Role: Running Both Men's and Women's Programs

At NIU, Stefko will fill a demanding dual assistant role under men’s head coach John Carlson and women’s head coach Kim Kester. While dual-program coaching structures are common in collegiate golf due to roster size and logistics, they require an assistant capable of managing distinct athletic cultures and schedules. Stefko's responsibilities will focus on daily practice structure, individual swing mechanics, recruiting support, and tournament travel management.

According to Stefko, the primary factor that drew him to DeKalb was the shared commitment of both Carlson and Kester to building a competitive yet supportive environment. For a Division I program, a dual assistant serves as the primary day-to-day connector between the head coaching staff and the roster. Stefko's collegiate history indicates he is well-prepared to manage this workload, ensuring both Huskies rosters receive consistent instruction throughout the season.

The Menlo Legacy: Driving Success Through Transitions

During his three-year tenure at Menlo College in California, Stefko demonstrated an ability to maintain competitive success during a major athletic transition. During his time with the Oaks, the athletic department shifted from NAIA status into NCAA Division II. Despite the heightened competition, Menlo's golf programs continued to win. In his final season, the men's squad secured two tournament victories and produced the PacWest Scholar-Athlete of the Year, while the women's team captured two titles and had two players earn All-PacWest honors.

His earlier campaigns at Menlo were equally productive. In 2023, the women's team won four tournaments, qualified for the NAIA national championship, and finished the year ranked in the national top 15, led by the country's top-ranked individual player. This consistent record of team wins and individual accolades was a key factor in making him an attractive candidate for the NIU coaching staff.

The DI Transition: Why This Hire Matters for the Huskies

For Northern Illinois University, adding an assistant with collegiate experience is a practical, low-risk move. Division I golf programs operate under intense logistical and travel pressures, and having an assistant who understands the rhythm of collegiate competition is highly valuable. Both Carlson and Kester highlighted Stefko’s professionalism, positive energy, and past coaching success as the key reasons for his hire.

The true value of an assistant coach in college golf is often quiet. They handle the recruiting calls, manage the practice facility details, organize travel itineraries, and provide the individual, fine-tuned instruction that head coaches cannot always fit into their schedules. Whether Stefko's presence results in immediate tournament trophies remains to be seen, but his résumé aligns perfectly with what a modern Division I program requires.

The Raw Read: Pragmatic Infrastructure Over Flashy Announcements

Ryan Stefko is not a household name, and his hiring as an assistant coach will not make national headlines. However, college golf programs are built on these quiet, structural additions. This is a pragmatic hire of a young coach with a clear record of player development stepping up from NAIA and Division II success into the Division I ranks.

The success of this appointment will not be measured by the announcement itself, but by the recruiting classes Stefko helps secure and the stroke-average improvements of the Huskies' players over the next few seasons. By bringing in an experienced dual-program developer, NIU has secured a practical building block for its golf programs. If Stefko can replicate his Menlo results in DeKalb, the Huskies have made a very smart addition.
